# ADR-001: Detector / licence posture (AGPL Ultralytics vs permissive-only)

- **Status:** Proposed — documented-open (unresolved)
- **Date:** 2026-07-07
- **Decision owner:** TrafficPulse project lead (maintainer role)
- **Decision deadline:** before the first detector-integration unit of Phase 1
- **Supersedes:** none
- **References:** `docs/architecture-review.md` §10, §11, §27

## Context

TrafficPulse needs an object detector (and adjacent model/tracker/OCR stack). The
choice carries a licensing posture that this ADR keeps **explicitly open**. The
architecture review (§11) deliberately left it unresolved because it is a genuine
project decision about *future reuse and distribution*, not a technical default.

Five distinct licence concerns must **not** be treated as interchangeable:

1. **Framework / source-code licence** (the detector library's code);
2. **Model-weights licence** (pretrained weights, which may differ from the code);
3. **Dataset licence** (training/eval data — a model/framework licence never
   grants rights to dataset content);
4. **Derived-artifact licence** (whether trained weights inherit the framework's
   terms);
5. **Deployment / distribution implications** (research prototype now vs any
   future commercial or closed-source distribution).

This ADR records facts and options only. It makes **no legal claim beyond
authoritative licence text and is not legal advice.**

## Decision drivers

- Development velocity for a time-boxed academic capstone.
- Freedom to reuse/redistribute later (and any future commercial path).
- Keeping the detector behind the U2 perception contract so a later swap stays
  bounded in cost.
- Honest licence hygiene (no dataset-download or use before licence review —
  see U4 registry).

## Considered options

- **Option A — Ultralytics (AGPL-3.0) for velocity.** Fastest path; commit to an
  AGPL public repository; keep the detector behind the perception contract so a
  permissive migration remains bounded. Ultralytics' stated position is that
  trained weights inherit AGPL [K — confirm before reliance]; a separate
  commercial Enterprise licence exists to bypass AGPL.
- **Option B — permissive-only stack.** RT-DETR / D-FINE (Apache-2.0) + timm
  (Apache-2.0) + ByteTrack / OC-SORT (MIT) + PaddleOCR (Apache-2.0). More
  integration effort; no AGPL coupling.
- **Option C — defer / keep open** (this ADR's current state): proceed with
  detector-independent Phase 1 work while the decision is pending.

## Decision

**OPEN — no decision is made in Phase 0-F.** A recommendation is *recorded but not
adopted*: architecture-review §11 leans to Option A (Ultralytics for velocity,
AGPL public repo, detector behind the contract) with a bounded permissive
migration path. This remains a recommendation only; the decision owner resolves
it by the decision deadline above.

## Consequences

- **Detector integration is BLOCKED** in Phase 1 until this ADR is resolved.
- **Detector-independent Phase 1 work is NOT blocked** — geometry utilities, the
  synthetic track generator, rule-engine foundations, and contracts-driven wiring
  may proceed while this ADR is open.
- The perception contract (U2 `Detection`) is the seam that keeps a later
  detector swap bounded regardless of which option is chosen.

## Risks and mitigations

- **AGPL coupling of trained weights** [K]: if Option A is chosen, trained
  weights and any distributed service may fall under AGPL. *Mitigation:* commit
  to that posture explicitly, or choose Option B; keep the detector behind the
  contract either way.
- **Licence terms may change:** the facts below must be **re-verified from
  official sources before the decision is made**.
- **Dataset-rights confusion:** a framework/model licence does not grant rights
  to dataset content; dataset licences are governed separately by the U4 registry.

## Revisit triggers

- Before the first detector-integration unit of Phase 1 (hard deadline).
- Any change in deployment/distribution goals (e.g. closed-source or commercial
  distribution).
- Any change to the upstream framework or weights licence terms.
